About this mock

What you'll be tested on

The exam covers four scenario domains drawn from real architectural work with Claude. Each question is a small case study with one best answer — picked for the shape of the architectural decision, not for trivia.

Research pipelines

Multi-agent retrieval, citation grounding, sub-agent isolation, state restoration after partial runs.

Code exploration

Tool granularity, side-effect tiers, refactor invariants, replayable transcripts, prompt-injection defense.

Customer support

Intent routing, PII boundaries, refund-tool authority, brand voice, hand-off payloads, latency.

Extraction pipelines

Schema versioning, evidence-span grounding, idempotency, sampling-based audit, two-tier model routing.

For partner companies

The official certification path

This mock is independent. The real CCAF credential is awarded by Anthropic to architects at partner organizations who complete the official learning path and pass the proctored exam.

Apply to the Claude Partner Network

Your company submits a partnership application; partners get access to the Claude Academy for their teams.

Get Academy access

Once approved, request access with your company email and you'll be enrolled in the learning path.

Complete the learning path

Work through the official modules: prompt design, tool use, multi-agent orchestration, evaluation, safety.

Validate cohort completion

Partners typically validate completion across a small cohort of architects from the same organization.

Sit the proctored exam

60 questions, 120 minutes, single continuous session. Tab-switching and external resources are flagged.

Receive the credential

If you pass, you become a Claude Certified Architect — Foundations. Most credentials carry a renewal cycle.

Step 1 — pick a question set

Three sets, 180 questions total.

Three independent question banks. Sets 1 and 2 are parallel forms at standard difficulty. Set 3 is harder, with edge cases and subtler distractors. Pick "Random" to roll for variety on each attempt.

Step 2 — pick a mode

You'll attempt: Set 1

Use timed for a realistic dry run; untimed if you want to think through each one.
